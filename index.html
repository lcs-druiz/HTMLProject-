San Francisco 49ERS

The San Francisco 49ers are a professional American football team based in the San Francisco Bay Area. They compete in the National Football League (NFL) as a member of the league's National Football Conference (NFC) West division. The team plays its home games at Levi's Stadium in Santa Clara, California. The 49ers won five Super Bowl championships between 1981 and 1994. Four of those came in the 1980s and were led by Hall of Famers Joe Montana, Jerry Rice, Ronnie Lott, Steve Young, Charles Haley, Fred Dean and coaches Bill Walsh and George Seifert. They've played in 7 Super Bowls total which is ranked 3rd all time in the NFL. They have been division champions 20 times between 1970 and 2019, making them one of the most successful teams in NFL history. The 49ers rank 4th all time in playoff wins (32) and have been in the league playoffs 27 times: 26 times in the NFL and one time in the AAFC. They've also played in the most NFC Championship games (16) hosting 10 of them, also an NFC record.

The 49ers have several players inducted in the Hall of Fame, some of the best players in the HOF that played or coached the 49ers are:

  1. Joe Montana (QB)
  2. Jerry Rice (WR)
  3. Bill Walsh (HC)
  4. Ronnie Lott (DB)
  5. Steve Young (QB)
  6. Deion Sanders (DB)
  7. Terrel Owens (WR)

49ERS Super Bowl History:

  1. Super Bowl XVI: 49ers 26, Bengals 21
  2. Super Bowl XIX: 49ers 38, Dolphins 16
  3. Super Bowl XXIII: 49ers 20, Bengals 16
  4. Super Bowl XXIV: 49ers 55, Broncos 10
  5. Super Bowl XXIX: 49ers 49, Chargers 26
  6. Super Bowl XLVII: Ravens 34, 49ers 31
  7. Super Bowl LIV: Chiefs 31, 49ers 20

One of the most famous moments in not just 49ers history but the whole NFL is the so called "The Catch" a catch made by Dwight Clark off a pass from Joe Montana to beat the Cowboys and go to the Super Bowl.
